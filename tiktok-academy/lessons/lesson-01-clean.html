The ecosystem: 4 ways to shop & sell

TikTok Shop has four touchpoints where shoppers find and buy products — and each one is a different doorway for you to earn. Shoppable Videos are normal TikToks with a product link attached. Shop Page is a dedicated shopping tab inside the app where people browse like they would on Amazon. Live Shopping is where you sell during a LIVE stream in real time. How you get paid: the 4 revenue models

There are four ways to actually put money in your bank from TikTok Shop — and they're stackable. The Affiliate Model is performance-based: you add a product link to a video and earn 5–20% commission when people buy. No inventory, no shipping, no faff. The UGC Model is retainer-based: brands pay you a flat fee (usually £50–£200+ per video) to create content for them, and you get paid whether or not the video sells anything. The Hybrid Model is the best of both worlds — a flat creation fee plus commission on sales. And Live Shopping is real-time selling during a broadcast, where you earn commission on every sale made while you're live.

Performance vs. retainer — pick your lane

Here's the important distinction most people miss. When you do Affiliate (Performance), you're the salesperson — your income varies based on how many people actually buy. The ceiling is unlimited (a single viral video could pay rent), but you only eat what you kill. When you do UGC (Retainer), you're the content creator — brands pay you a fixed fee per deliverable, and you're paid for the asset, not the outcome. Start with Affiliate because you can do it today, by yourself, with no gatekeepers. Upgrade to UGC retainer deals once you've built a portfolio — that's what agency connections like Creator Scans unlock.

What is "binding"?

"Binding" is the word TikTok uses when you officially link your creator account to an agency — in this case, Creator Scans. It's basically saying: "this agency is my designated partner for TikTok Shop." It creates an exclusive relationship for Shop content, the contract sets out how long and how the commission splits work, and — crucially — you can unlink if you ever need to switch. You're part of the team, but you're not trapped.

The TAP advantage

This is why binding maths out. TAP (TikTok Affiliate Partner) is a marketplace where brands post campaigns with higher commission rates that are only available to agency-bound creators. Independent creators literally cannot see these deals. As an example: a brand might offer 10% commission on its public listing, but 17% on the same product through a TAP campaign. That's a 70% pay rise on the exact same sale, just because you're bound. Over a year of consistent selling, that gap becomes huge.

The reality check: input vs. output

This graph is the single most important slide in this lesson. Your input (showing up, posting consistently, improving) is a flat, steady line from day one. Your output (actual sales) is basically zero for the first few months… and then it starts curving up steeply around month 6–9. Focus on the input — the output is lagging behind, but it's coming.
