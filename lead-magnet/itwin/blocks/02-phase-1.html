Phase 1 of 5

Build Your Asset Library

Before any AI tool can create a convincing version of you, it needs raw material. This phase is a one-time setup. Do it properly once and you will reuse these assets for every product you ever promote.

Step 1 — The 20 to 30 Rule

You need 20 to 30 clear photos of yourself from different angles. Two sets get the best results.

Studio set + lifestyle set — 20 to 30 photos total

  • Studio set (15 to 20): plain wall or green screen, plain clothing, slight angle variations
  • Lifestyle set (10): natural photos around your home, same plain clothing rule
  • No filters, no sunglasses, no other people in frame
Why plain clothing? It makes it trivial for the AI to swap outfits later — essential when you are producing ads across different product categories.

Step 2 — Background Plates

Give your twin real places to "film" in. Photograph your actual home so the AI does not invent fake-looking rooms.

Stand in each corner, use your 0.5x lens, shoot every room that matches your products

Selling beauty? Shoot the bathroom. Kitchen gadgets? Counter and stove. Fashion? Bedroom, hallway, garden, driveway.

Step 3 — Reference Videos (2 minutes each)

Record four short videos of you talking to camera. The AI uses them to learn your mouth, head, and natural gestures.

  • Sitting, landscape — 2 minutes
  • Sitting, portrait — 2 minutes
  • Standing, landscape — 2 minutes
  • Standing, portrait — 2 minutes
Pro tip: be exaggeratedly expressive with your mouth. It is what lets the AI lip-sync perfectly to new scripts later.

Step 4 — Clone Your Voice

Your twin needs to sound like you. Two routes depending on how much realism you want.

Speed route: record a clean 30 to 60 second sample, upload to ElevenLabs, type scripts from then on.

Realism route: record each voiceover manually and upload it straight into HeyGen or Fal.ai. Best for hero ads.

Best of both: use the ElevenLabs clone for rapid testing, manual voiceover for the hero ads you scale.
